‚ö° The Pre-Trade Bias Checklist (2 minutes before every trade)
Print this and keep it visible:

Before ANY DeFi investment, ask yourself:

‚ñ° Am I seeking information that confirms what I want to believe? (Confirmation Bias)

‚ñ° Is this decision driven by recent price movements or social media? (FOMO/Recency Bias)

‚ñ° Am I risking more than my predetermined position size limits? (Overconfidence)

‚ñ° Have I considered equally credible opposing viewpoints? (Confirmation Bias)

‚ñ° Is my timeline realistic given market cycles? (Recency Bias)

‚ñ° Am I holding a loser hoping for recovery? (Loss Aversion)

‚ñ° Am I chasing because others are making money? (FOMO/Herd Mentality)

‚ñ° Am I anchored to purchase price or ATH instead of current reality? (Anchoring Bias)

If you answered YES to any question, STOP and address that bias before proceeding.

Livermore's Most Expensive Biases

Overconfidence After Success
After major wins, Livermore increased position sizes dangerously.

Your Protection: After significant gains, maintain or reduce position sizes for next 3 trades.

Commitment: "After winning more than $_____ in a single trade, I will not increase position sizes for 3 trades."

Loss Aversion in Cotton
Livermore held losing cotton positions far too long, hoping for recovery.

Your Protection: Set stops before entry, honor them without exception.

Commitment: "I will set stop losses before every entry and exit at stops without hesitation or hope."

Confirmation Bias with Tips
Livermore sometimes sought tips confirming his existing positions.

Your Protection: Research to disprove your thesis, not confirm it.

Commitment: "Before adding to any position, I will find 3 credible sources arguing against it."
